Introduction

My name is Sarah Norris. I am 26 years old, a mother, and the proud founder of Sarah’s Pet Stop LLC, a business I started from my deep love and passion for animals.

I live in Long Valley, New Jersey, and provide pet care services across Morris County and surrounding areas. As a Pet Sitter and Dog Walker, I have the responsibility of ensuring that pets under my care receive the best possible treatment, whether it’s providing comfort to a high-energy dog, easing the anxieties of a nervous animal, or simply making sure they are well-fed, exercised, and happy. My role extends beyond just taking care of pets—it’s about building trust, forming bonds, and providing personalized care that addresses each animal's unique needs. Being a Pet Sitter and Dog Walker requires a deep love for animals, patience, and a keen understanding of their unique needs. From providing daily walks and meals to offering companionship and behavioral training, I ensure that pets under my care feel loved, happy, and well-looked-after. Every animal deserves the best care, and it is my promise to give them that—whether it's through consistent feeding schedules, regular grooming, or the extra attention they need to overcome anxiety or high energy. By building strong, trusting relationships with each pet, I not only fulfill my responsibilities but also create a positive, caring environment that enhances their lives and their owners' peace of mind Pet sitting and dog walking is not just a service—it’s a relationship built on trust, care, and mutual respect. I approach each job with the understanding that every pet is unique and requires tailored attention. My dedication to providing the best care makes all the difference in ensuring that pets are happy and healthy while their owners are away

One of the most important aspects of my job is to create a bond with each pet I care for. Every animal has its own personality, and forming trust with them is crucial for their comfort and well-being. This is especially true for dogs with anxiety or high energy. By spending quality time with the pets and offering patience and reassurance, I am able to build a relationship where they feel safe and secure. Dogs with separation anxiety, for example, need extra attention, and my approach is always gentle, using positive reinforcement to help them feel more at ease. Similarly, high-energy dogs require structured routines to keep them calm, happy, and well-exercised.

For me, being a Pet Sitter and Dog Walker isn’t just about fulfilling a set of tasks—it’s about providing love and care to animals who rely on us for their happiness and well-being. I take pride in knowing that every pet I care for is treated with the utmost respect and attention. It is a job built on trust, affection, and responsibility, and every task I complete is done with the intent to make the pet feel loved and secure. Ensuring that pets are not just physically cared for but also emotionally supported is key to my approach. For example, during the walk or playtime, I take into consideration their mood, energy levels, and specific needs, making sure to adjust accordingly. Whether it’s a quiet stroll for a shy dog or an energetic run for a playful one, I always make sure the pet's needs are prioritized.
